Articles


Article 24 of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (UNCRPD) specifically addresses the right to education for individuals with disabilities. The UNCRPD is an international treaty that sets out the rights of persons with disabilities and emphasizes their inclusion and full participation in society. Here are the key points outlined in Article 24:

1. Equal Right to Education: Individuals with disabilities have the right to education on an equal basis with others, including in mainstream educational settings.

2. Inclusive Education: States Parties to the convention recognize the right of persons with disabilities to access an inclusive, quality, and free primary and secondary education on an equal basis with others in the communities in which they live. This means that individuals with disabilities should be able to participate fully in regular education settings alongside their peers without disabilities.


Webinar for Disabled People


Webinars can help people with disabilities in a variety of ways, including:
1. Raising awareness : Webinars can raise awareness about physical disabilities and their impact on life activities, and highlight the role of assistive technology.
2. Ensuring access : Webinars can help ensure that all students, regardless of their abilities, have access to high-quality educational opportunities and can actively participate in their learning journey.
3. Inclusion : Webinars can be a simple step towards inclusion, which is the process of involving people with disabilities in an active social life.
